How to watch WWE Royal Rumble 2023 in Australia

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  220 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Get ready to rumble.

WWE’s annual TV special Royal Rumble – which has been running since 1988 – is set to hit screens around the world tomorrow.

For Australian fans of the long-running professional wrestling franchise, you’d no doubt be aware the WWE Network was discontinued locally on January 23.

Last September, it was announced The Foxtel Group* would be the exclusive home of all WWE content, meaning the only way to tune into Royal Rumble is on their platforms, including Binge.

In some good news, the previously pay-per-view TV event can be streamed for free by Binge subscribers.

Stream Royal Rumble on BINGE. New customers get a 14-day free trial and start streaming instantly. Sign up at binge.com.au

Royal Rumble, which always airs in January, is one of WWE’s “big five” events of the year, alongside the likes of WrestleMania, SummerSlam, Survivor Series, and Money in the Bank.

More Coverage

The Royal Rumble sees a number of wrestlers, usually 30, compete to be the last man/woman standing, with the aim to eliminate their competitors by throwing them over the ring rope.

WWE legend Stone Cold Steve Austin holds the record for the most Royal Rumble victories with three, which he achieved in 1997, 1998 and 2001.
