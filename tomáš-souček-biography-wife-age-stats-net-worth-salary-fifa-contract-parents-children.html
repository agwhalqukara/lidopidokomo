Career

Tomáš Souček started his professional football career in his home country of the Czech Republic, playing for Vlašim in 2014. He then moved to Slavia Prague in 2016, where he became an important player for the team. 

During his time at Slavia, Tomás assisted the team in winning two Czech First League titles and two Czech Cups. Additionally, he participated in European competitions like the Europa League and Champions League, allowing him to showcase his skills on a larger platform.

Tomáš Souček‘s career took an exciting turn in January 2020 when he joined on loan with West Ham United in the English Premier League, with the option to purchase. He quickly adapted to the rigors of English football and established himself as a crucial member of West Ham’s midfield. He rapidly garnered respect among fans due to his powerful presence on the field, unwavering work ethic, and ability to score vital goals.

During the summer of 2020, West Ham exercised their purchase option, ensuring that Souček would become a permanent squad member. Throughout the 2020-21 season, Souček showcased his exceptional skills and developed a strong partnership with Declan Rice in the central midfield position. 

He contributed significantly to the team’s success and emerged as one of West Ham’s top scorers alongside Michail Antonio, netting an impressive 10 goals. As a result of his remarkable performances, Tomáš Souček was honored with the highly esteemed Hammer of the Year award, a recognition bestowed upon him by the club’s passionate fans.

Souček‘s triumphant journey with West Ham continued into the 2022-23 campaign. His contribution was pivotal as they advanced to the final of the Europa Conference League, marking their first appearance in a European final in over four decades. Ultimately, West Ham emerged as the champions, prevailing over Fiorentina with a 2-1 victory in Prague, and it was Souček proudly hoisted the trophy as their captain.

Following his debut in 2016, Tomáš Souček has become a prominent figure in the Czech Republic national team, showcasing his leadership abilities. Notably, he was appointed as captain of the team in 2021 after the retirement of Vladimír Darida.
